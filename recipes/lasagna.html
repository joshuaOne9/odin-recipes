Lasagna

Lasagna is a classic Italian comfort food made by layering broad, flat pasta sheets with alternating layers of sauce, cheese, and often meat or vegetables, then baking everything together in the oven until bubbly and golden. The sauce is typically a slow-simmered meat ragù or tomato-based marinara, while the cheese layers feature creamy ricotta (mixed with egg to help it set), stretchy mozzarella, and a sharp Parmesan or Pecorino finish on top. It's assembled in a baking dish starting with a thin layer of sauce on the bottom, then repeating the pattern of noodles, sauce, and cheese for two to three layers.

Ingredients

  • 1 box of lasagna noodles
  • 1-1.5lb ground beef
  • 1 jar marinara sauce
  • 15 oz ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup Parmesan cheese
  • 2-4 eggs depending on size of dish
  • Parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F.
  2. Boil the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions.
  3. Cook the ground beef in a large skillet until browned.
  4. Mix the cooked beef with the marinara sauce.
  5. Remove noodles from the pot and lay flat on foil paper to cool off. We are not layering them yet.
  6. Mix eggs, ricotta cheese, mozzarella cheese, salt, pepper, and parsley in a bowl. Mix well. Afterwards, it should look like a creamy mixture of cheese.
  7. We are going to start layering. So don't use all of your ingredients yet when making the first layer. Even them out. Grab a long pan and start layering. First, layer the noodles at the bottom. Next, layer the meat over the noodles. Then again, layer another layer of noodles on top of the meat. Finally, we'll layer the bowl of cheese mixture that we made earlier on top of the noodles. Lastly, the very top layer will be a layer of meat, then noodles, and then the cheese mixture again. We'll add a final sprinkle of the Parmesan cheese on top.
  8. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until the cheese is bubbly and golden.
