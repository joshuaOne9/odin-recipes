Lasagna

Lasagna is a classic Italian comfort food made by layering broad, flat pasta sheets with alternating layers of sauce, cheese, and often meat or vegetables, then baking everything together in the oven until bubbly and golden. The sauce is typically a slow-simmered meat ragù or tomato-based marinara, while the cheese layers feature creamy ricotta (mixed with egg to help it set), stretchy mozzarella, and a sharp Parmesan or Pecorino finish on top. It's assembled in a baking dish starting with a thin layer of sauce on the bottom, then repeating the pattern of noodles, sauce, and cheese for two to three layers.
