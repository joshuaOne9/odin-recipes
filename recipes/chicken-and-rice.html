Chicken and Rice

This Chicken and Rice is a simple, budget-friendly comfort meal where boneless, skinless chicken thighs are coated in a flavorful blend of paprika, oregano, thyme, garlic powder, and onion powder, then seared until golden brown in a deep skillet. After removing the chicken, diced onion is sautéed in the same pan to pick up all those delicious browned bits, followed by toasting the long-grain white rice for a minute or two before pouring in vegetable broth. The chicken goes back on top of the rice, the lid goes on, and everything simmers together on low heat for 20 minutes — no peeking allowed — followed by a 5-minute rest. The result is perfectly cooked, fluffy rice infused with all the savory seasonings and chicken juices, topped with tender, juicy chicken thighs and a sprinkle of fresh parsley.

Ingredients

  • 2 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp pepper
  • 1.25 lbs boneless, skinless chicken thighs (4-5 thighs)
  • 2 Tbsp cooking oil, divided
  • 1 yellow onion, diced
  • 1 cup long-grain white rice (uncooked)
  • 1 3/4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 Tbsp chopped parsley (optional garnish)

Steps

  1. Season the chicken: Combine the paprika, oregano, thyme, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper in a small bowl. Coat both sides of the chicken thighs with the seasoning mix.
  2. Sear the chicken: Add 1 Tbsp cooking oil to a deep skillet over medium heat. Once hot, add the chicken thighs and cook for a few minutes on each side until well browned. The chicken doesn't need to be cooked through yet. Remove to a clean plate.
  3. Sauté the onion: Reduce heat to medium-low, add the remaining 1 Tbsp oil and the diced onion. Sauté for about 5 minutes until softened, letting the moisture from the onion dissolve the browned bits from the skillet.
  4. Toast the rice: Add the uncooked rice to the skillet and sauté with the onion for 1-2 minutes to lightly toast it.
  5. Add broth: Pour in the vegetable broth and stir briefly to dissolve any remaining browned bits from the bottom of the skillet.
  6. Simmer: Return the chicken to the skillet on top of the rice. Place a lid on the skillet, turn heat to medium-high, and bring to a full boil. Once boiling, reduce heat to low and simmer for 20 minutes — do not lift the lid or stir during this time.
  7. Rest: After 20 minutes, turn off the heat and let it rest (lid still on) for an additional 5 minutes.
  8. Serve: Remove the lid, fluff the rice with a fork, and stir the onions back in. Garnish with chopped parsley and enjoy!
