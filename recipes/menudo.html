Menudo

Menudo "Rojo"

A traditional Mexican soup made with tripe and hominy. What makes it "rojo" is the addition of red chili peppers, which gives the soup its distinctive red color and a rich, spicy flavor. For extra flavor, add some cilantor and use limes or lemons to squeeze on top of the soup. When choosing the tripe, look for pieces that are clean and have a good amount of fate, as this will add flavor and tenderness to the soup, preferably honeycomb tripe, which has a unique texture that absorbs the flavors of the broth well. Menudo is often enjoyed as a hearty and comforting dish, especially during colder months of the year!

Ingredients

  • 3-4 pounds of beef or honeycomb tripe. Depends on how many people you are serving. Cut into 1-2 inch pieces. Should be bite-sized.
  • 1- 2 heads of garlic. Again depends on how many people you are serving.
  • 2-2.5 teaspoons of cumin.
  • 2-3 tablespoons of Mexican style chili powder. Depends on how much you like it spicy.
  • 1-2 teaspoons of oregano.
  • 1-2 teaspoons of salt. Depends on your taste. I prefer to add a lot more salt than the average person.
  • 1-2 teaspoons of pepper.
  • 1 gallon of water.
  • 1 large 6lbs 14ozcan of Juanita's Mexican Style hominy. Bush's Golden Hominy also works and is good if you cannot find the traditional variety.
  • 1-2 tablespoons of olive oil. This is optional.
