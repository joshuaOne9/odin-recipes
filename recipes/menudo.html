Menudo

Menudo "Rojo"

A traditional Mexican soup made with tripe and hominy. What makes it "rojo" is the addition of red chili peppers, which gives the soup its distinctive red color and a rich, spicy flavor. For extra flavor, add some cilantro and use limes or lemons to squeeze on top of the soup. When choosing the tripe, look for pieces that are clean and have a good amount of fat, as this will add flavor and tenderness to the soup, preferably honeycomb tripe, which has a unique texture that absorbs the flavors of the broth well. Menudo is often enjoyed as a hearty and comforting dish, especially during colder months of the year!

Ingredients

  • 3-4 pounds of beef or honeycomb tripe. Depends on how many people you are serving.
  • 1- 2 heads of garlic. Again depends on how many people you are serving.
  • 2-2.5 teaspoons of cumin.
  • 2-3 tablespoons of Mexican style chili powder. Depends on how much you like it spicy. I like a lot more spice in mine.
  • 1-2 teaspoons of oregano.
  • 1-2 teaspoons of salt. Depends on your taste. I prefer to add a lot more salt than the average person.
  • 1-2 teaspoons of pepper.
  • 1 gallon of water.
  • 1 large 6lbs 14ozcan of Juanita's Mexican Style hominy. Bush's Golden Hominy also works and is good if you cannot find the traditional variety.
  • Cilantro, lemons, limes, and onions. These are all optional toppings and will be added to the soup after it is served.

Steps

  1. Clean the tripe by rinsing it thoroughly under cold water and removing any excess fat.
  2. Trim and cut the tripe into bite-sized pieces.
  3. Place the tripe in a large pot and add the gallon of water. Make sure there is enough water to cover the tripe.
  4. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 2 hours.
  5. Grind or mash the garlic, cumin, and chili powder together into a molcajete, preferably. If you don't have one, grind or mash the spices together well and add them to the pot with the tripe. Add oregano, salt, and pepper.
  6. Once the tripe is tender, add the hominy and any additional seasonings to the pot that you prefer. Depending on the water level, you may need to add more water to ensure the soup has enough broth. Simmer for an additional 1-2 hours to allow the flavors to meld together.
  7. Adjust the seasoning to your preferred taste and continue to let it simmer until you feel and taste that it is ready to serve. The tripe should be really tender and the broth should be flavorful and spicy. If you want a thicker broth, you can mash some of the hominy to release its starches, which will help thicken the soup.
  8. Enjoy your delicious bowl of menudo! This is where you can add any extra toppings you like, such as limes or lemons. Some people prefer to add some chopped cilantro and onions on top as well.
